I am a first-year PhD student at Pompeu Fabra University (UPF) (Department of Translation and Language Sciences), under the supervision of Gemma Boleda. My research field is Computational Semantics and Pragmatics.

In my current research, I investigate the influence of contextual information on word interpretation and generation, using the frameworks of Distributional Semantics and Deep Learning. I am part of the team working on the ERC project "AMORE: A distributional MOdel of Reference to Entities", which focuses on grounding language into reality through a computational model of linguistic reference. I am member of the UPF research groups COLT (Computational Linguistics and Linguistic Theory) and GLiF (Formal Linguistics Group).

Before starting my PhD, I obtained a BA in Digital Humanities from the University of Pisa (2012-2015) and a MSc in Logic from the University of Amsterdam (2015-2017). During this time, I carried out research on metaphors, referring expressions in dialogue and negated adjectives.
